CONTEXT

I have been developing games since 2014, starting with the development
software GameMaker and progressing up to the use of the Unity engine to
work on some of the projects you're looking at below. I've worked on genres
ranging from first-person games to the focus of education. Interested? Take
a look at some of the things I've done in the past.

PAST PROJECTS


2008


Before I had any access to a computer, I would use my parents laptop
to try to figure out how to make games. Considering my age, I had no
experience in playing video games apart from occasionally playing Mario
on the GameBoy Advance. Regardless, I was determined to seek an answer,
at which I came to a limit of resources considering how tools used today
were brand new at the time. Whether it was Unity, or even Unreal Engine,
at the time, it wasn't easy to access such software, nor did I have the
tools to install them. But, regardless, I was able to come across a few
websites that offered subscriptions to make games. I did not have enough
money or knowledge at the time to know what that meant, though, I managed
to access the websites built-in engine to throw together assets and make
myself a platformer. It wasn't anything big at the time, but the simple task
of making a simple game intrigued me, though it had not crossed my mind for
several years after that.

2014


Alzerath

Around the summer of 2014, thanks to the increasingly growing gaming community
on YouTube around the same time period, I was inspired to make a deep dive into
game development myself. Since I had access to a computer, I was able to use tools
that were more up to date at the time. Ultimately, since I couldn't pay for a game
engine, I landed on a free trial of GameMaker Studio. This time, I had a goal in mind,
at which I wanted to develop a video game inspired by Megaman, set in a medieval world.
This was an inspiration I had after the game 'Shovel Knight' was released, henceforth
I tried my best to pursue the task in mind. After watching videos on putting together
sprite animations, and programming collisions between objects, I was able to create
a very early version of the game, which unfortunately is lost as of now as the file
was corrupted in the middle of a file transfer. Regardless, it was the spark that brought
me to help make video games today. The game title 'Alzerath' was somewhat random, but
could have been inspired by medieval board games and how the characters were named.


2015-2019


SHIFT.exe

After taking a year off to focus on more important things such as education, I eventually spent
the summer of 2015 building a game based on another inspiration I had. At the time, sites such as
itch.io, and GitHub had really hit the new medium for how games and software were shared. Although
I did have money to purchase games, one particular game came at no cost, it was called 'Sonic Ball'.
The use of Unity to make such a title inspired me to get into learning the basics of the engine,
and frankly I had just the PC to download and build projects on top of it.
It was widely held popular amongst my social circle at the time, and some peers even offered to
help me out with its development. The project, with major gaps in production between the years,
had eventually reached a certain version of itself that was somehwat playable, but it had not
fit to my expectations. Regardless, without having to work at a company, I still managed to
collaborate with others who had similar initiatives as I did at the time. Below, is the very
first version of the game, I will remaster the final version soon.


Lost in NY

During this time period, I had created another Unity game for which its files were also corrupted.
But frankly, it is clear enough in my memory to explain its premise. After going on a brief road trip
to Manhattan, I was inspired eventually build a top-view mobile game about someone who entered
New York City, but was lost in the city so much that they had to find a way out. The game was really
inspired by the struggle to navigate what felt like such a new city to me, and the level was built around
that feeling through a maze-structured version of New York. The game was incomplete due to a lack of textures,
and just time in general to fix such issues. Regardless, it was one of my other inspired projects that I may
consider rebuilding sometime for release.
